## Search Operators and Tips

### Effective search patterns
```
"AI" site:techcrunch.com after:YYYY-MM-DD
"raised" "$" "AI" site:crunchbase.com
"AI" "Series A" OR "Series B" site:venturebeat.com
"agent" OR "agentic" AI 2025 site:a16z.com
```

### Reddit search
```
site:reddit.com/r/artificial "AI" new tool
site:reddit.com/r/LocalLLaMA release
```

### GitHub
```
site:github.com/trending "AI" OR "LLM"
```

### Signal filters
- Prioritise sources from the **last 7 days** for trends, **last 30 days** for funding
- Funding rounds under $2M are early signal; over $50M is mainstream confirmation
- GitHub repos with >500 stars in <2 weeks = strong developer traction signal
- Product launches that hit #1 on Product Hunt = strong early-adopter signal

## EngineAI Context (for opportunity scoring)

When evaluating opportunities, consider EngineAI's position:

**Current strengths:**
- AI skills and automation platform
- Existing customer relationships in NZ/APAC market
- Strong Claude Code / agentic AI expertise
- Speed of execution (small team advantage)
- Ability to ship in weeks, not quarters

**Target customers:**
- SMEs and mid-market companies in NZ and APAC
- Teams wanting to embed AI into workflows without in-house ML
- Businesses that need custom AI solutions, not generic SaaS

**Strategic filters — an opportunity scores higher if it:**
- Can be built on top of existing Claude/Anthropic capabilities
- Solves a workflow problem for non-technical end users
- Has a clear NZ or APAC first-mover advantage
- Generates recurring revenue (not one-off projects)
- Can be packaged as a repeatable product vs a bespoke service

**Strategic filters — an opportunity scores lower if it:**
- Requires training proprietary models from scratch
- Needs a large sales team or enterprise procurement cycle
- Is dominated by well-funded US players with 12+ month head start
- Has no clear distribution advantage for EngineAI
